Field Operations Manual

1. Mission & Core Principles

Mission: To deter crime and protect property by observing, documenting, and reporting suspicious or illegal activity.

Core Principles:

  • Visibility – Even in plain clothes, our presence deters bad actors.
  • Discretion – We blend in, gather information quietly, and avoid unnecessary confrontation.
  • Accuracy – Every report must be clear, factual, and supported with evidence.
  • Safety First – Never engage physically; call police for intervention.

2. Scope of Work

We do not act as armed or unarmed guards. We:

  • Patrol properties on foot or in unmarked vehicles.
  • Monitor camera feeds and live property activity.
  • Document suspicious behavior with photos, videos, and detailed notes.
  • Relay information to property owners, HOAs, or police.
  • Follow up with written incident reports.

3. Field Conduct Rules

DO:

  • Wear plain clothes that match the environment.
  • Carry discreet identification (watch, lanyard, or pin) recognizable only to staff/clients.
  • Record time/date/location of all observations.
  • Stay in public or authorized-access areas.
  • Maintain calm, non-threatening body language.

DON’T:

  • Wear uniforms resembling police/security.
  • Carry weapons, batons, or handcuffs.
  • Detain, chase, or physically block anyone.
  • Verbally threaten or intimidate.
  • Enter private areas without written permission from the owner.

4. Patrol Protocol

  1. Pre-Shift:
    • Check in with operations via secure messaging app.
    • Confirm patrol route and any priority watch areas.
    • Ensure all equipment is charged and functioning.
  2. On Patrol:
    • Walk or drive the property in a casual, non-patterned route to avoid predictability.
    • Take discreet notes on activity.
    • Use bodycam or phone camera for any incidents.
    • Maintain situational awareness — note entrances, exits, lighting, and traffic flow.
  3. If Suspicious Activity Is Seen:
    • Do not approach.
    • Take photo/video evidence from a safe distance.
    • Note:
      • Date/time
      • Exact location
      • Description of person/vehicle
      • Activity observed
    • Contact police if a crime is in progress.
  4. End of Shift:
    • File incident reports (even if nothing happened — “clear patrol” logs are important).
    • Upload all evidence to the secure storage system.

5. Incident Reporting

Required Report Elements:

  • Header: Date, time, location, observer’s name.
  • Incident Description: Factual, chronological account. Avoid assumptions or emotional language.
  • Evidence: Photo/video links, vehicle plates, witness statements (if available).
  • Action Taken: Notified police? Informed property owner? Continued observation?

Example:

02/18/2025 – 8:42 PM – North lot, Desert View Mall
Observed male approx. 30–35 yrs, wearing black hoodie & jeans, attempting to open parked vehicles.
Vehicle plate: AZ 7XZ-349.
Contacted Tucson PD non-emergency line at 8:44 PM.
Uploaded 3 photos & 2 short videos to system.

6. Equipment Guidelines

  • Smartphone with secure reporting app
  • Covert bodycam (pen cam, button cam, or glasses cam)
  • Discreet ID item (watch, pin, or lanyard)
  • Small flashlight
  • Notebook & pen
  • Portable charger/power bank

Optional:

  • GPS tracker in vehicle for route verification
  • Night vision monocular for low-light areas

7. Police Interaction Protocol

  • Always identify yourself as “Neighborhood Watch Surveillance – contracted by the property owner”.
  • Never claim to be “security” or “law enforcement.”
  • Provide factual information only — no speculation.
  • Offer evidence (photos/video) in a professional format.

8. Legal Safeguards

  • Operate only with written contracts granting property access.
  • Clearly state in all contracts:
    “Neighborhood Watch Surveillance provides observation and reporting only. We do not detain, confront, or physically engage with individuals.”
  • Keep all reports and video evidence securely stored for at least 12 months.
  • Train all personnel annually on legal limits and privacy laws.

9. Client Communication

  • Provide weekly or monthly summary reports.
  • Immediate alerts for urgent situations (crime in progress, safety hazard, property damage).
  • Offer video evidence packages for insurance or legal purposes.

10. Company Branding

  • Plainclothes presence with subtle identifiers.
  • Business cards with name, title (“Surveillance Technician”), and phone/email.
  • Public tagline: “We see what others miss.”
